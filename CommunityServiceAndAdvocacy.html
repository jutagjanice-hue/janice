Community Service and Advocacy

My Advocacy

In a world driven by technology—where everything from education to healthcare, from business to entertainment, depends on innovation—Computer Science students are the builders of tomorrow. Yet too often, we face challenges that limit our potential: outdated equipment, limited internship opportunities, rapid changes in technology that our curriculum struggles to keep up with, and the pressure of workloads that test our mental health.

We are not asking for privilege— We are asking for the resources that match the responsibilities placed upon us.

If we want to produce world-class programmers, engineers, cybersecurity experts, data scientists, and researchers, we must first create world-class learning environments. That means modern laboratories, reliable internet, access to cloud services and industry-standard tools. It means a curriculum that evolves as fast as technology does—one that includes AI, cybersecurity, data science, mobile development, and all the fields shaping the digital era.

But resources are not enough. We need opportunities.

We need stronger partnerships with companies, more internships, mentorship programs, career guidance, and platforms where students can showcase their talent. Computer Science students shouldn’t just graduate with a diploma; they should graduate with experience, confidence, and readiness for the real world.

And as we advocate for academic growth, we must not forget mental health. CS students often deal with complex projects, long hours of coding, deadlines, and the pressure to constantly learn something new. Supporting mental health is not optional—it is essential.

Most importantly, we must advocate for inclusion. The tech world is diverse, and our classrooms should reflect that diversity. Women, LGBTQ+ students, students with disabilities, and underrepresented groups deserve equal encouragement and support in this field.

So today, let this be our message:

Empower the students who will engineer the future. Equip the learners who will build the systems we will depend on. Support the minds who will shape the digital world.

Let us work together—administrators, educators, policymakers, and students—to create an environment where every CS student can learn, innovate, and thrive.

Because when we invest in Computer Science students, we are investing in the future of our society.

Thank you, and may we continue to code, create, and empower one another
